Before: MARTIN and GRIFFIN, Circuit Judges; DUGGAN,* District Judge

       PATRICK J. DUGGAN, District Judge. This matter was submitted to the panel on briefs

on March 30, 2010, with arguments addressing the merits of the underlying order of the National

Labor Relations Board (“the Board”). On June 17, 2010, however, the United States Supreme Court

issued its decision in New Process Steel, L.P. v. NLRB, No. 081457, holding that the Board lacks

authorization to act when it has fewer than three current members. There is no dispute that the

underlying order in this case was issued at a time when there were only two members on the Board.1
